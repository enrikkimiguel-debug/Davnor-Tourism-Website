About the Province

Davao del Norte

Capital: Tagum City Davao Region (XI) Most Populous in DavReg Banana Capital of PH

Location. Davao del Norte is located in the Davao Region (Region XI) in Mindanao, Philippines. It is bounded by Davao del Sur to the south, Compostela Valley to the east, Bukidnon to the north, and the Davao Gulf to the west. The province also encompasses Samal Island to the south in Davao Gulf.

History. The province was created on May 8, 1967 through Republic Act 4867 authored by Congressman Lorenzo S. Sarmiento, Sr., when the original mother Province of Davao was divided into three: Davao del Norte, Davao del Sur, and Davao Oriental. The three provinces celebrate their founding anniversary every July 1. Its first governor, Hon. Verulo C. Boiser, served for ten years starting from the province's creation.

Known Attractions. Davao del Norte is home to the stunning Island Garden City of Samal — a world-class beach destination — as well as Mt. Apo's foothills, pristine waterfalls, lush wildlife sanctuaries, and the vibrant city of Tagum, the provincial capital known for music and culture.

Reasons to Come

Why Visit Davao del Norte?

Whether you're a beach lover, a foodie, a history buff, or a thrill-seeker — Davao del Norte has something extraordinary waiting for you.

01
World-Class Beach Destination

Samal Island's pristine white-sand beaches and coral-rich waters rank among the finest in Southeast Asia. Dive, snorkel, or simply relax in paradise.

02
Rich Indigenous Heritage

Experience authentic Lumad and Manobo culture — vibrant dances, traditional music, handwoven textiles, and indigenous crafts preserved for generations.

03
Adventure in Nature

Trek pristine waterfalls, explore hidden caves, summit scenic peaks, and kayak along crystal rivers across the province's diverse natural landscapes.

04
Tropical Food Paradise

Taste exotic fruits fresh from the farm — durian, rambutan, mangosteen, and countless varieties of banana — alongside fresh seafood and hearty Mindanaoan dishes.

05
Vibrant Festivals

The Musikahan sa Tagum and Panagtagbo Festival bring the province alive with music, dance, and community spirit — a feast for the senses and the soul.

06
Safe & Accessible

Well-connected through Tagum City and the Francisco Bangoy International Airport in Davao City, the province is easy to reach and proud of its warm Mindanaoan hospitality.

Before You Go

Travel Tips

Make the most of your Davao del Norte visit with these essential tips for every type of traveler.

Best Time to Visit

December to May is the ideal season — dry weather, calm seas, and best visibility for diving and snorkeling. Peak festival season runs February to August with the Musikahan sa Tagum and Panagtagbo Festival.

Transportation Options

Fly into Francisco Bangoy International Airport in Davao City, then take a bus or van to Tagum City (1–1.5 hrs). Ferries to Samal Island depart from Sasa Wharf every few minutes for only ₱10–₱25.

Safety Reminders

Always wear a life jacket on boat rides. Never trek remote trails alone — hire a licensed local guide. Check weather forecasts before beach or mountain activities, especially June to October.

Budget & Currency

Most beaches, markets, and attractions are very affordable. Bring cash — ATMs are limited on Samal Island and in remote municipalities. Budget ₱1,500–₱3,000/day for a comfortable visit.

What to Pack

Bring reef-safe sunscreen, insect repellent, a waterproof bag, light breathable clothing, and sturdy water shoes for waterfall hikes. A power bank is essential in remote areas with limited electricity.

Respect Local Culture

Ask permission before photographing indigenous peoples in traditional attire. Follow Leave No Trace principles at natural sites — take only photos, leave only footprints. Support local vendors and guides.
