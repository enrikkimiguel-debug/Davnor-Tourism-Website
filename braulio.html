Municipality of
Braulio E. Dujali

Davao del Norte's youngest municipality — a thriving banana heartland where agricultural innovation and community progress go hand in hand.

Municipality Overview

Braulio E. Dujali, Davao del Norte

Davao del Norte ~100 km² Area Banana Producing Town Growing Community

Braulio E. Dujali is the youngest and one of the smallest municipalities in Davao del Norte, officially established in 2000. Named after the late Governor Braulio E. Dujali, this municipality has rapidly developed into a vibrant agricultural community, known primarily for its extensive banana plantations that form the backbone of the local economy.

Situated strategically within the Davao Region, Braulio E. Dujali is a compact but productive municipality where modern agri-business meets close-knit community living. Its proximity to major urban centers makes it accessible while retaining the charm and tranquility of rural Mindanao — an emerging destination for those who seek an authentic provincial experience.

Banana Capital Heartland

Vast Cavendish banana plantations stretch across the municipality, supplying major export markets and driving economic growth.

Youngest Municipality

Established in 2000, Braulio E. Dujali represents Davao del Norte's most recent addition and fastest-growing community.

Agri-Business Hub

Home to large-scale agri-business ventures and packing houses serving both domestic supply chains and international export.

Close-Knit Community

A warm, welcoming population composed of Cebuano settlers and indigenous communities living in harmony and shared progress.

Geography & Area

Fertile Lowlands of
Davao del Norte

Braulio E. Dujali occupies a compact but agriculturally rich territory in the heart of Davao del Norte. Its flat to gently rolling terrain makes it exceptionally suited for large-scale plantation farming, and the land is crisscrossed by irrigation canals and farm roads that serve the thriving banana industry.

The municipality borders several other towns in Davao del Norte, positioned close to the regional center of Tagum City, which provides access to markets, trade hubs, banking, and government services. This central location gives Braulio E. Dujali a competitive advantage in logistics and trade connectivity.

Despite its small land area, the municipality's highly productive soil yields consistently high agricultural output, making it a vital contributor to Davao del Norte's status as the Banana Capital of the Philippines and a key player in the country's export economy.

Brief History

From Barangay to Municipality:
A Story of Progress

The territory now known as Braulio E. Dujali was once part of the municipalities of Carmen and Sto. Tomas. Its separation and establishment as an independent municipality on October 30, 2000 — through Republic Act No. 8807 — was a direct result of the community's growing population, economic development, and the need for localized governance.

The municipality was named in honor of Governor Braulio E. Dujali, a beloved statesman who dedicated his career to the advancement of Davao del Norte. His legacy continues to inspire the municipality's drive for progress, good governance, and inclusive development for all its residents.

In its relatively short existence, the municipality has grown rapidly — from a cluster of farming barangays to a recognized administrative unit with its own local government, infrastructure projects, and socio-economic programs that continually improve the quality of life of its constituents.

Demographics & Language

A Unified Community
Rooted in Mindanao Culture

The population of Braulio E. Dujali is primarily composed of Cebuano (Bisaya) speakers, the descendants of settlers who migrated from the Visayas throughout the mid-20th century. Indigenous communities, including the Manobo peoples, also maintain a presence in the municipality's upland and rural areas.

Cebuano is the dominant language spoken at home, in the markets, and in everyday social settings. Tagalog and English are widely understood and used in formal, educational, and business contexts, facilitating smooth interactions for visitors, investors, and government workers from across the Philippines.

The municipality's social character is shaped by the shared values of hard work, community cooperation, and respect for elders — qualities cultivated through generations of agricultural life and deeply ingrained in the local culture of Davao del Norte.

Climate & Environment

A Lush Agricultural Landscape
All Year Round

Like much of Davao del Norte, Braulio E. Dujali enjoys a Type II tropical climate — characterized by no pronounced dry season and abundant rainfall distributed throughout the year. This consistent precipitation creates ideal growing conditions for banana cultivation and other tropical crops that thrive in warm, humid environments.

The local government has been proactive in promoting environmentally responsible farming, encouraging plantation operators and smallholders alike to adopt sustainable practices such as integrated pest management, proper waste disposal, and the protection of waterways adjacent to farmlands.

The municipality's verdant farmscapes — rows upon rows of banana plants, interspersed with coconut trees and rice paddies — give the land a lush, pastoral beauty that reflects the abundance of Mindanao's natural environment and the diligence of its farming communities.
