Municipality of
Santo Tomas

The agricultural and industrial powerhouse of Davao del Norte — where vast banana plantations meet a thriving modern economy.

Municipality Overview

Santo Tomas,
Davao del Norte

Davao del Norte 396 km² Area Industrial Hub Banana & Coconut Capital

Santo Tomas is one of the most progressive municipalities in Davao del Norte — a key player in agriculture, agribusiness, and industrial development. Known for its vast banana and coconut plantations, the town is also home to processing plants, commercial hubs, and a strong entrepreneurial spirit that drives consistent growth.

With its expanding economy and modern infrastructure, Santo Tomas continues to attract investors, businesses, and visitors looking for a balance between productive rural landscapes and the conveniences of modern urban development — making it one of the most dynamic communities in the province.

Banana Plantations

Vast banana and coconut farms supplying national and international markets with premium tropical produce.

Industrial Growth

Processing plants, agri-industrial enterprises, and commercial hubs that drive the town's economic dynamism.

Strategic Location

Close proximity to both Tagum City and Davao City makes Santo Tomas a prime business and transit hub.

Diverse Community

A multicultural population of Cebuano, Tagalog, Manobo, and Visayan communities living and working in harmony.

Geography & Area

A Land of Fertile Fields
and Expanding Industries

Santo Tomas covers 396 square kilometers of economically significant terrain, featuring fertile agricultural plains, small mountain ranges, and designated industrial zones. This diverse geography allows the municipality to host both large-scale farming and modern manufacturing activities side by side.

Its proximity to Tagum City and Davao City — the two major urban centers of the Davao Region — makes Santo Tomas an exceptionally attractive location for trade, business expansion, and commercial development. The town sits at a strategic crossroads that facilitates the movement of goods, services, and people.

The combination of productive agricultural land and excellent regional connectivity has made Santo Tomas one of the most economically vibrant municipalities in the province, with steady growth driven by both agribusiness and industrial development.

Brief History

From Farmlands
to an Economic Hub

Santo Tomas began as a rural farming community where indigenous Manobo tribes and Visayan settlers coexisted, shaping the early social and agricultural character of the town. These communities developed the land's agricultural potential through decades of dedicated cultivation and community building.

Over the years, banana plantations, coconut farms, and agri-industrial enterprises transformed the municipality into a progressive economic center. The development of processing facilities and commercial infrastructure helped elevate Santo Tomas from a quiet farming town to a recognized industrial hub in Davao del Norte.

Today the municipality is known for its booming agribusiness sector and modern infrastructure — a legacy that honors its agricultural roots while embracing the opportunities of contemporary economic development and regional connectivity.

Demographics & Language

A Diverse and
Business-Oriented Community

The majority of Santo Tomas' population speaks Cebuano (Bisaya), with a significant mix of Tagalog and English speakers — particularly in business, education, and government sectors. This linguistic accessibility makes the municipality welcoming to entrepreneurs, investors, and workers from across the Philippines.

The town's multicultural makeup reflects decades of migration and cultural exchange, bringing together Visayan, Tagalog, Manobo, and other communities whose combined efforts have built Santo Tomas into the progressive municipality it is today.

This diversity of backgrounds and perspectives continues to fuel the community's entrepreneurial energy and forward-looking mindset — making Santo Tomas an attractive destination for those who want to be part of a growing and vibrant economic community.

Climate & Environment

Sustaining Growth While
Protecting Natural Resources

Santo Tomas enjoys a tropical monsoon climate with consistent rainfall that sustains its agricultural productivity year-round. The municipality's farming calendar is closely aligned with these seasonal patterns, ensuring optimal crop yields across all its major agricultural sectors — from bananas and coconuts to rice and vegetables.

The local government promotes sustainable farming practices, responsible industrial zoning, and green environmental initiatives — ensuring that environmental management keeps pace with the town's rapid economic growth and that natural resources are protected for future generations.

These efforts reflect Santo Tomas' commitment to building prosperity that does not come at the expense of its natural heritage — a balance that sets a positive example for other fast-growing municipalities across the Davao Region.
