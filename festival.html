Festivals & Celebrations
in the Davao Region

Music & Arts Indigenous Culture Thanksgiving Festivals Year-Round Events

The Davao Region is home to some of the most colorful and culturally significant festivals in the Philippines. These events showcase the rich traditions of indigenous groups, agricultural abundance, and religious heritage — making them a must-experience for both local and international visitors.

From the grand Kadayawan Festival in Davao City to unique town fiestas and tribal gatherings, each celebration highlights the region's deep-rooted history and dynamic culture. Every festival is a living expression of the people's identity, gratitude, and pride.

Kadayawan Festival

Grand Celebration of
Culture & Nature's Bounty

The Kadayawan Festival is an annual thanksgiving and cultural celebration held in Davao City, Philippines, dedicated to honoring the city's 11 ethnolinguistic indigenous tribes, the bounties of nature, and the region's rich heritage. The name is derived from the Mandaya word madayaw, meaning "good, valuable, or superior."

Officially renamed from "Apo Duwaling" in 1988 by then-Mayor Rodrigo Duterte to reflect indigenous gratitude, the festival has grown into one of the grandest cultural events in Mindanao — featuring street dancing, floral float parades, indigenous rituals, tribal performances, and agricultural exhibits.

Kadayawan is not just a spectacle — it is a profound expression of gratitude to the land, to the indigenous peoples who have stewarded it, and to the diverse communities that have made Davao Region what it is today.

Araw ng Davao / Dabaw

Month-Long Anniversary
of a Proud City

Araw ng Dabaw is the month-long founding anniversary and charter day celebration of Davao City, held annually in March to honor the city's history, heritage, and multicultural unity. The city was officially inaugurated on March 1, 1937.

The primary holiday was moved from March 16 to March 1 pursuant to Republic Act No. 11379 in 2019. The month-long celebration includes historical exhibits, cultural performances, civic events, trade fairs, and community activities that draw participants from across the region.

Araw ng Dabaw is a proud reminder of Davao City's remarkable journey from a small settlement to one of the Philippines' most dynamic and beloved cities — a celebration every visitor to the region should experience.

Musikahan Festival

Music Capital
of the South

The Musikahan Festival is a vibrant annual celebration of music and arts that takes place in Tagum City, Davao del Norte — a city proudly recognized as the "Music Capital of the South." This dynamic festival showcases a rich tapestry of musical performances, artistic exhibitions, and cultural activities.

The festival draws artists and enthusiasts from across the region and beyond, with recent editions highlighting its growing ambition: the 23rd edition is slated for February 2026, following the 22nd edition held from February 17–23, 2025. These events serve as a testament to Tagum City's dedication to fostering a thriving arts and culture scene.

Musikahan is more than a festival — it is Tagum City's declaration of identity, a celebration of its deep love for music, and an open invitation to the world to experience the creative spirit of the South.

Bulawan Festival

Davao de Oro's
Golden Celebration

The Bulawan Festival is an annual celebration in Davao de Oro (formerly Compostela Valley) that honors the province's founding anniversary and its rich gold-mining industry. The festival is typically held every March 8, marking a proud provincial tradition.

The 19th Bulawan Festival and 28th Founding Anniversary took place from February 28 to March 8, 2026 — a nine-day celebration featuring the Bulawanong Pasundayag street dance competition, a Jewelry Expo, and a forum on tourism and investment that showcased the province's golden cultural heritage.

The Bulawan Festival is a shining example of how a region's economic identity can be transformed into a vibrant cultural celebration — drawing visitors, investors, and artists together under the banner of gold.
