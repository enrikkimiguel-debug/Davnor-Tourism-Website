Municipality of
San Isidro

Land of "brown gold," hidden caves, and cascading waterfalls — an eco-cultural gem rising fast in Davao del Norte.

Municipality Overview

San Isidro (Sawata),
Davao del Norte

Davao del Norte 5th Class Municipality Eco-Tourism Destination Pop. 27,233 (2020)

San Isidro — now officially named Sawata — is a 5th-class municipality in Davao del Norte known for its rich eco-cultural wonders. Created on June 26, 2004 through Republic Act No. 9265, it has rapidly grown into one of the province's premier eco-tourism destinations, offering adventure, thrill, learning, and authentic cultural discovery.

Beyond its world-class cacao (tableya) industry, San Isidro captivates visitors with stunning waterfalls, intriguing cave systems, and pristine natural landscapes — a complete eco-adventure package that is entirely its own and unlike anywhere else in the province.

Tableya / Cacao

San Isidro is famous for its "brown gold" — tableya — cacao-based chocolate discs that are the pride of the local agri-industry.

Twin Waterfalls

Tagtugunan and Sambulawan Falls offer spectacular cascades surrounded by lush virgin forest and natural crystal pools.

Cave Exploration

Kabyawan Cave in Dakudao offers world-class spelunking and guided underground exploration experiences.

Eco-Tourism Hub

One of the province's fastest-rising eco-destinations, combining natural wonders with authentic cultural immersion.

Tableya / Cacao

The "Brown Gold"
of San Isidro

San Isidro is most celebrated for its tableya — round, finished cacao products made from dried and roasted cacao beans that serve as the key ingredient in traditional Filipino hot chocolate. The municipality's cacao industry has put it firmly on the map as a premium producer of high-quality chocolate raw materials.

Beyond its progressing cacao economy, eco-tourism sites flourish throughout the municipality. Visitors increasingly combine cacao farm tours with waterfall treks and cave explorations — creating a unique agri-eco experience that is completely and authentically San Isidro's own.

The cacao industry has also empowered local farming families, providing a sustainable and value-added crop that connects the rural community directly to premium chocolate markets both domestically and internationally.

Tagtugunan Falls

Serene Waters Amid
Virgin Forest

Tagtugunan Falls features a very deep catchment pool surrounded by lush forest trees and vines, offering a magical retreat into untouched nature. The falls are accessible by trail and reward hikers with the sight and sound of rushing water tumbling into a crystal-clear pool of stunning natural beauty.

Visitors often wade across the deep pool to stand directly beneath the cascading water — an exhilarating and deeply refreshing experience that has become the standout highlight of any trip to San Isidro. The cool, clean water and shaded forest surroundings make Tagtugunan a perfect escape.

Swimming, nature photography, and forest trekking are the most popular activities here — and the falls' relatively unspoiled setting means visitors can enjoy a genuine sense of wilderness discovery that is becoming increasingly rare.

Sambulawan Falls

A Three-Tiered
Natural Masterpiece

Sambulawan Falls is a magnificent three-tiered waterfall and one of the most prominent natural attractions in San Isidro. Named after the beautiful Sambulawan tree species that flourishes in the surrounding forest, the falls add a layer of botanical charm to their already stunning visual impact.

Situated near Kabyawan Cave and Tagtugunan Falls, Sambulawan forms part of a rich eco-tourism corridor within the municipality. Its multi-tiered structure creates a series of cascading pools, each offering its own unique perspective of the falling water.

San Isidro's eco-tourism reputation is built in large part on sites like Sambulawan — natural wonders that are extraordinary in their own right, yet accessible enough to welcome both experienced trekkers and first-time nature visitors with equal warmth.

Kabyawan Cave

Underground Wonders
in Dakudao

Kabyawan Cave, situated in Dakudao, San Isidro, is a notable destination for underground exploration — offering unique opportunities for spelunking and discovering the subterranean wonders of the region. The cave system features remarkable rock formations and passages that reward the adventurous explorer.

Recognizing its significant potential, Kabyawan Cave has become an important site for training skilled caving guides — programs that ensure exploration is conducted safely and responsibly, and that the cave's delicate ecosystem is properly managed and preserved for future generations.

Together with Tagtugunan Falls, Sambulawan Falls, and the tableya agri-industry, Kabyawan Cave completes San Isidro's identity as a full-package eco-tourism destination — one of the most exciting rising stars in the Davao del Norte travel landscape.
